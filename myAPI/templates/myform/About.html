What is churn?

Churn refers to the ratio of the number of members you lost in a given period to the total number of members. For example, if you have 1000 members and 10 of your members canceled their subscription that month, your churn speed corresponds to 10/1000, or 1%.

The action to be taken at this point is to identify the reasons for losing members. Churn may either be due to poor quality service, or a competitor may have started selling the same service on more attractive subscription terms.



Churn Prediction/ Modelling

This problem is usually concerned with predicting whether a member is going to continue with a company or not. This is very useful financially for companies as it helps them to target member groups. This is typically a binary classification problem. That is you need to look at the data and predict whether the person exits(1) or the person continues(0).

The data set which I have considered for modelling contains details of a bank's customers/ members and the target variable is a binary variable reflecting the fact whether the member left the bank (closed his account) or he continues to be a member.

The columns in this dataset are represented as follow :

  • Surname : The surname of the member
  • CreditScore : The credit score of the member
  • Geography : The country of the member(Germany/ France/ Spain)
  • Gender : The gender of the member(Female/ Male)
  • Age : The age of the member
  • Tenure : The member's number of years in the bank
  • Balance : The member's account balance
  • NumOfProducts : The number of bank products that the member uses
  • HasCrCard : Does the member has a card? (0=No,1=Yes)
  • IsActiveMember : Does the member has an active membership (0=No,1=Yes)
  • EstimatedSalary : The estimated salary of the member
  • Exited : Churned or not? (0=No,1=Yes)

Meaning of CreditScore term :

  • CreditScore : It is a scoring system created by looking at a person's credit history and various risk factors. This scoring system is the first criterion that banks check in your loan application and is the most important criterion in terms of loan evaluation. For a score with a range between 350-850.
